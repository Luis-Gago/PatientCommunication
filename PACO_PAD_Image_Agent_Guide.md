### Ankle brachial index (ABI)
**Source:** NIH  
**Description:** The ankle-brachial index (ABI) test is usually the first test used to diagnose PAD. The test compares blood pressure in your ankle with the blood pressure in your arm. Your provider uses a blood pressure cuff and  ultrasound  device for this painless test. A healthy ABI result is 1.00 or greater. If you have an ABI of less than 0.90 while resting, you may have PAD. An ABI of less than 0.40 is a sign of severe PAD.

### Stages of PAD
**Source:** Created with biorender  
**Description:** PAD can take different forms. You may have reduced or absent pulses in the feet. You may have claudication, which is leg pain, soreness, or tiredness when you walk that resolves with rest. You can have rest pain, which is pain in the feet that occurs when you are not walking. It can wake up from sleep at night. You might also notice sores on your feet that heal slowly or do not heal. Gangrene is caused by tissue death when the lack of blood flow is severe.
